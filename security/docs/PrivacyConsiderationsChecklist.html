*Note:

Personal information means recorded information about an identifiable individual other than business contact information. The individual may be an employee, a contractor, a participant, an applicant, or a client. If the personal information is de-identified using an indirect identifier such as Drivers’ License; Correctional Number; application, case, and file number; Username, etc.., the information is still considered personal information. Indirect Identifiers may identify an individual when they are connected with other pieces of information to single out an individual (e.g. age, gender, date of visit). While indirect identifiers on their own may not be personal information, they are considered personal information if they can be combined together to identify an individual. This is commonly referred to as the mosaic effect - when the combinations of data elements that in isolation look relatively innocuous, can amount to personal information.
